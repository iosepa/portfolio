About

I started programming in 2002. My professor said he’d give an automatic “A” to any student who could build a VB6 calculator in 15 lines of code. I did, but no auto-A! Surprise, surprise.

Later I worked as a programmer. My final two years there I was lead. If I couldn't fix it, no one did.

After graduating I became an engineer at NASA. It was interesting, just not as engaging. I missed the creativity and constant problem solving in web development. So, here I am, ready to create, develop, and help you make the web (and world) a better place.

"To code or not to code? Is that even a question?”
